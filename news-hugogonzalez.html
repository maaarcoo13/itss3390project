Former Real Madrid shooting guard Hugo González experienced his first NBA Media Day as a member of the Boston Celtics, marking a major milestone in his promising basketball career. Selected with the 28th overall pick in the first round of the 2025 NBA Draft, González arrived in Boston with both excitement and humility, ready to earn his place in one of the league’s most historic franchises.

At just 19 years old, the Madrid native expressed his gratitude for the opportunity to represent such a prestigious team. “It’s a dream come true. Growing up in Spain, I always watched the NBA, and now I’m here, wearing a Celtics jersey. I just want to work hard, learn from my teammates, and help the team in any way I can,” said González during his first press appearance at the Auerbach Center.

Known for his defensive intensity, athleticism, and ability to finish above the rim, González had already made waves in Europe playing for Real Madrid’s senior team in both the Liga ACB and EuroLeague, where he stood out as one of the top young talents. His transition to the NBA has generated optimism among Celtics fans, who see him as a long-term investment and potential two-way contributor.

During Media Day, Hugo shared moments with teammates such as Jayson Tatum, Jaylen Brown, and Jrue Holiday, as well as Head Coach Joe Mazzulla, who praised the young Spaniard’s attitude and work ethic. “Hugo brings energy, focus, and a team-first mentality. He’s young, but he’s already showing the maturity we value in this organization,” said Mazzulla.

While the road ahead will be challenging, González’s combination of European experience, defensive versatility, and relentless drive could make him a valuable asset for the reigning Eastern Conference contenders. As the preseason begins, all eyes will be on the former Real Madrid standout as he adapts to the speed, strength, and spotlight of the NBA.
