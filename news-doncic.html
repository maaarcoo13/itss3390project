Luka Dončić has entered the 2025-26 NBA season in peak form — lean, focused, and ready to dominate in his new home: the Los Angeles Lakers. Arriving at training camp in the best shape since his sophomore year, the Slovenian superstar has generated massive excitement across the league, as the Lakers look to build a new era around him.

After being traded from Dallas last summer, Dončić made a clear commitment to fitness and conditioning, showing up noticeably slimmer and quicker. “This is the best shape I’ve seen him in,” said Lakers Head Coach JJ Redick. “He’s more explosive, more vocal, and completely invested in leading this team.”

The 26-year-old guard, now entering his eighth NBA season, is expected to carry a heavier playmaking load alongside Anthony Davis and new signing Austin Reaves. Early scrimmages have already shown improved pace and chemistry, with Dončić running the offense efficiently and embracing a leadership role both on and off the floor.

“This summer was all about getting better — physically and mentally,” Dončić said. “I wanted to come in ready to set the tone. The Lakers are about winning, and that’s why I’m here.”

Fans and analysts are already calling this version “Skinny Luka 2.0,” comparing his energy and movement to his breakout 2020 campaign. With his refined conditioning, improved defense, and unmatched court vision, Dončić looks ready to deliver a historic season in purple and gold.

If his preseason form is any indication, Luka Dončić could be on the verge of an MVP-level year — and the Lakers might once again be serious championship contenders.
